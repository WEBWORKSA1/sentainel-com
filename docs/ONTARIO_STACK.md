## Stacking Examples

### Toronto hospital using AI scribe (e.g., Nuance DAX)

Simultaneously subject to:
- **PHIPA + IPC AI Scribe Guidance** — direct healthcare AI rules
- **Ontario Human Rights Code** — discrimination via biased clinical AI
- **MFIPPA Bill 97** — if part of municipal public hospital system
- **EDSTA** — if a public sector hospital under FIPPA
- **Bill 149 ESA s. 8.4** — if 25+ employees and AI in hiring
- **Federal PIPEDA / CPPA** — automated decision-making rules
- **Trustworthy AI Framework** — flowed through provincial contracts

**Seven simultaneous obligation streams from one AI tool.** No
competitor handles this. Sentainel does.

### Mississauga municipality deploying a resident chatbot

Simultaneously subject to:
- **MFIPPA Bill 97 PIA mandate** — mandatory PIA before deployment
- **MFIPPA Bill 97 safeguards** — encryption, access controls, vendor
  oversight
- **MFIPPA Bill 97 breach reporting** — if PI exposed via chatbot
- **EDSTA** — AI accountability framework (when regulations land)
- **Ontario Human Rights Code** — if chatbot produces discriminatory
  responses
- **AODA** — WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility for chatbot interface
- **Trustworthy AI Framework** — flowed down from provincial
  procurement standards
- **Bill 149 ESA** — if used in municipal hiring (not this case, but
  same employer might be)

**Eight simultaneous obligation streams.** Municipalities will have
zero capacity to manage this themselves. Sentainel becomes essential.
