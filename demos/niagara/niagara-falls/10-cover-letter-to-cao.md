## Why I built it

Between December 2024 and January 2026, Ontario crystallized a four-layer AI regulatory stack that directly applies to your municipality. The IPC and the Ontario Human Rights Commission jointly published Principles for the Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence on January 21, 2026, and the IPC has stated these principles will "ground our assessment" of any AI use under MFIPPA. MFIPPA Bill 97 amendments come into force on July 1, 2026 and January 1, 2027.

Most Ontario municipalities have not yet operationalized a compliance posture against this stack. The City of Niagara Falls is in this position — and there is already a named, dated, public-record incident on file. Your General Manager of Municipal Works presented a sidewalk winter maintenance report to council in April 2026 that cited ChatGPT findings. He correctly disclaimed that the findings were "not a scientific survey." That single statement was both honest and important. It is also exactly the kind of incident that an IPC investigation will cite as evidence of unmanaged AI use if any future AI deployment by the City becomes the subject of a complaint.

This is not a criticism. Almost every Ontario municipality has equivalent exposure today. The difference is that the City of Niagara Falls happens to have its incident on the public record, in Canada's National Observer.

I built this package to demonstrate what a credible compliance posture looks like for your city specifically — not a template, not a checklist, but a deep, audit-ready bundle built from your actual public-record AI exposures.
