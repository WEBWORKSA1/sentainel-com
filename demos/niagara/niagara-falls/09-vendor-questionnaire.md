# AI Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire — City of Niagara Falls
## Pre-Procurement + Renewal Questionnaire for AI-Containing Products and Services

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance · MFIPPA Bill 97 + IPC-OHRC + Trustworthy AI Framework aligned**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## How to use

Issue this questionnaire to any vendor whose product or service contains AI components, before contract signature and at renewal. The vendor's responses become part of the procurement file and contribute to the City's MFIPPA Bill 97 PIA and IPC-OHRC governance record.

For vendors providing products with embedded generative AI (e.g., Microsoft 365 Copilot, Adobe AI features), this questionnaire may be partially answered by vendor public documentation; the procurement officer should compile public responses and only request direct vendor responses for City-specific gaps.

## Section B: IPC-OHRC Principle 1 — Valid and Reliable

**B1.** What independent testing has the AI component undergone? Provide certifications, audit reports, or testing methodology.

**B2.** What is the published accuracy / performance metric for the AI component in the use case applicable to the City?

**B3.** How often is performance re-evaluated? Provide the cadence and any public reporting.

**B4.** Has the AI been tested for performance across Ontario's diverse populations (linguistic, racial, age, ability)?

**B5.** What is the vendor's commitment to maintaining validity across the contract term?

---

## Section C: IPC-OHRC Principle 2 — Safe

**C1.** Describe the AI safety monitoring approach. Include real-time, periodic, and post-incident monitoring.

**C2.** Under what conditions will the vendor pause or decommission the AI component?

**C3.** What is the vendor's incident response procedure if the AI begins producing unsafe outputs?

**C4.** Has the vendor decommissioned any version of this AI product in the past 24 months? If yes, describe.

**C5.** What is the vendor's commitment regarding model updates that may materially change AI behaviour during the contract term?

---

## Section D: IPC-OHRC Principle 3 — Privacy Protective

**D1.** Where is City personal information stored? (data residency)

**D2.** Where is the AI processing performed? (Canada, US, EU, other)

**D3.** Is the AI processing in Canada (i.e., subject to PIPEDA / MFIPPA / provincial law) or out of Canada?

**D4.** If out of Canada, what cross-border safeguards apply? (SCCs, etc.)

**D5.** Does the AI use City personal information for any purpose other than service provision to the City? (training, improvement, derivative products)

**D6.** Does the AI use City personal information to train or fine-tune the vendor's models? (yes/no — critical)

**D7.** Provide your Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) where applicable.

**D8.** What privacy-enhancing technologies (de-identification, differential privacy, synthetic data) are applied to City data?

**D9.** What is the data retention period for AI processing logs containing City personal information?

**D10.** Can the City request deletion of all City personal information from the AI environment? Describe the procedure.

---

## Section E: IPC-OHRC Principle 4 — Human Rights Affirming

**E1.** Has the AI been audited for disparate impact on Ontario Human Rights Code-protected grounds? Provide audit report.

**E2.** What categories of decisions does the AI inform or automate, and which are subject to human review?

**E3.** What is the vendor's procedure if the City identifies a discriminatory output?

**E4.** Does the AI use any feature that could function as a proxy for Code-protected grounds (postal code, name, photo, voice)? If yes, what controls?

---

## Section F: IPC-OHRC Principle 5 — Transparent

**F1.** Provide a plain-language description of how the AI component operates, suitable for inclusion in the City's public AI inventory.

**F2.** Can the AI explain its outputs in language accessible to non-experts? (yes/no/limited — describe)

**F3.** Can the vendor produce traceability documentation (training data, hyperparameters, monitoring logs) on request from the City or a regulator?

**F4.** Is the AI's use disclosed to the end-user (City resident or staff) at the point of interaction? Describe.

---

## Section G: IPC-OHRC Principle 6 — Accountable

**G1.** Does the vendor have a designated AI Governance Lead or equivalent? Provide name + role.

**G2.** What is the human-in-the-loop architecture for the AI component as deployed for the City?

**G3.** Provide the vendor's AI governance policy.

**G4.** What is the vendor's procedure for handling regulatory inquiries (IPC, OHRC, federal Privacy Commissioner) about the AI component?

---

## Section H: IPC-OHRC Principle 7 — Accountable Recourse

**H1.** What recourse mechanism is available to a City resident or staff member who wishes to challenge an AI output?

**H2.** Does the vendor have a whistleblower mechanism for vendor staff to report AI non-compliance?

**H3.** What is the vendor's procedure if a regulator (IPC, OHRC) requests cooperation with an investigation into the City's use of the AI?

---

## Section I: MFIPPA Bill 97 specific

**I1.** Does the vendor commit to assisting the City in completing a MFIPPA Bill 97 PIA for the AI component? Describe the assistance.

**I2.** Will the vendor produce a Privacy Impact Assessment that the City can rely on (or supplement)?

**I3.** Does the vendor's contract include MFIPPA breach notification obligations matching MFIPPA Bill 97 timing requirements (mandatory breach reporting to IPC)?

**I4.** Does the vendor's contract include cooperation obligations if the IPC issues a binding order affecting the City's use of the AI?

---

## Section J: Trustworthy AI Framework + NIST AI RMF

**J1.** Has the vendor mapped the AI component to NIST AI Risk Management Framework controls? Provide the mapping.

**J2.** Does the vendor's compliance posture align with Ontario's Responsible Use of AI Directive (Dec 1, 2024)? Describe.

**J3.** Does the vendor commit to maintaining NIST AI RMF alignment for the contract term?

---

## Section K: EDSTA Bill 194 readiness

**K1.** Is the vendor aware of Ontario's Enhancing Digital Security and Trust Act, 2024 (EDSTA) AI provisions, and is the vendor prepared to assist the City in compliance when regulations are made?

**K2.** Does the vendor's commercial pricing change if EDSTA proclamation occurs during the contract term? (i.e., are EDSTA-driven changes covered by current pricing?)

---

## Section L: Termination + transition

**L1.** What is the vendor's procedure for returning or destroying City data at contract end?

**L2.** What is the vendor's procedure for handing over AI configuration / customization / fine-tuning data to a successor vendor?

**L3.** What is the timeline for confirmed data destruction (with certificate)?

---

## Section M: Reference cities + comparable deployments

**M1.** List Ontario municipal customers using the same AI product. Provide reference contacts (subject to those customers' consent).

**M2.** Has any Ontario municipal customer of this product been subject to an IPC complaint or OHRC complaint involving this product? Describe outcome.

**M3.** Has this product been deployed in any Canadian regulated environment (PHIPA, FIPPA, federal Privacy Act)? Describe.

---

## Scoring + decision

The City's procurement officer scores each section on a 3-point scale:

- 2 = vendor response demonstrates strong compliance posture
- 1 = vendor response demonstrates partial compliance
- 0 = vendor response demonstrates non-compliance or insufficient detail

**Aggregate score thresholds:**

- 24+ / 26 sections at 2: vendor is procurement-ready
- 18–23 / 26 sections at 2: vendor procurement requires risk-acceptance memo + remediation plan
- < 18 / 26 sections at 2: vendor procurement not recommended without substantial remediation

The scoring methodology is published in advance to vendors so they can prepare comprehensive responses.
