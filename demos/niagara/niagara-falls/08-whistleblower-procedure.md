# Whistleblower Procedure — City of Niagara Falls
## Staff Reporting of AI Non-Compliance per IPC-OHRC p7 + MFIPPA Bill 97

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance · Implementation-ready procedure**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## Why this exists

MFIPPA Bill 97 amendments (effective January 1, 2027) include explicit whistleblower protection for City staff who report MFIPPA non-compliance. IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 states:

> "Members of institutions should be empowered through safe whistleblowing protections to report instances where an AI system does not comply with legal, technical, or policy requirements. Whistleblowers should be able to report non-compliance to an independent oversight body responsible for reviewing or overseeing the AI system, without fear of reprisal."

The City of Niagara Falls currently has general whistleblower protections under the Public Service of Ontario Act and the Municipal Act, but no AI-specific channel. This procedure adds one.

---

## Scope

This procedure protects City of Niagara Falls staff who report:

1. AI use in violation of City policy
2. AI use in violation of MFIPPA, IPC-OHRC Principles, or Ontario Human Rights Code
3. Suspected discriminatory AI outputs
4. Suspected privacy breaches involving AI systems
5. Failure to follow PIA / AIA procedures for new AI deployments
6. Vendor (e.g., Axon, OpenAI, Microsoft) failures affecting City AI systems

It applies to all permanent + temporary + contract staff, plus board appointees and elected officials.

---

## Channel design

### Internal channel (preferred)

Staff may report concerns directly to:

- **The AI Governance Lead** (once designated)
- **The City Clerk** (interim, until AI Governance Lead designated)

Methods: in person, via dedicated email (e.g., ai-concerns-confidential@niagarafalls.ca), or via City's existing whistleblower hotline.

### External channel

IPC-OHRC p7 requires that whistleblowers be able to report to an **independent oversight body**. For Ontario municipalities this is:

- **The Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario** for privacy / MFIPPA concerns: https://www.ipc.on.ca/en/about-ipc/contact-us
- **The Ontario Human Rights Commission** for discrimination concerns: https://www.ohrc.on.ca/en/contact-us
- **The Office of the Ontario Ombudsman** for general public-service concerns: https://www.ombudsman.on.ca/

The City **must not require** that staff exhaust internal channels before going external. The whistleblower has the right to choose.

---

## Anti-reprisal protection

Staff who report in good faith under this procedure are protected from:

- Termination, demotion, or suspension
- Reduction in salary or benefits
- Reassignment that constitutes reprisal
- Disciplinary action of any kind related to the report
- Harassment or retaliatory behaviour by colleagues or supervisors

**Bad-faith reports** (knowingly false, made to cause harm) are not protected and may be subject to discipline. The burden of demonstrating bad faith rests with the City.

---

## Confidentiality

The identity of a whistleblower is confidential to the extent possible. The AI Governance Lead may share the identity only with:

- The CAO (if escalated)
- The City Solicitor (for legal advice)
- The relevant external oversight body if the report is referred externally and the whistleblower consents

The whistleblower's identity must **not** be disclosed to the system owner, the subject of the report, or any colleague unless legally required.

---

## Response procedure

### Receipt

1. AI Governance Lead acknowledges receipt within 2 business days
2. AI Governance Lead conducts initial assessment within 10 business days

### Investigation

3. AI Governance Lead determines investigation scope + assigns investigator (may be internal HR / Audit / external)
4. Investigation is documented contemporaneously
5. Investigator interviews whistleblower, system owner, and other relevant parties
6. Investigation should conclude within 60 business days for most matters; 90 days for complex matters

### Disposition

7. Investigation produces one of three findings:
   - **Substantiated**: AI system or practice is non-compliant; corrective action initiated
   - **Partially substantiated**: some elements confirmed; partial corrective action
   - **Not substantiated**: no compliance issue found; whistleblower notified with reasons

8. The whistleblower is informed of the disposition within 5 business days of investigation conclusion (subject to any confidentiality restrictions of the investigation itself)

### Corrective action

9. If substantiated, the AI Governance Lead works with the system owner to implement corrective action
10. Corrective action is documented in the AI inventory + relevant PIA/AIA
11. If the matter is serious, the AI Governance Lead briefs the CAO and considers council disclosure

---

## Reporting obligations

The AI Governance Lead must produce a **quarterly whistleblower report** to the CAO that contains:

- Number of reports received (no identifying details)
- Category breakdown (policy / MFIPPA / IPC-OHRC / Code / vendor / other)
- Substantiation rate
- Corrective actions taken
- Any external referrals

The **aggregate annual statistics** (no case-level detail) are included in the City's annual AI compliance report to council per IPC-OHRC p5 transparency requirement.

---

## Integration with existing City policies

This procedure supplements, does not replace, the City's existing:

- General whistleblower / fraud reporting policy
- Workplace harassment policy
- Code of Conduct for Members of Council
- Conflict of Interest policy

Where a concern overlaps with multiple policies, the most protective interpretation applies.

---

## Training requirement

All City staff receive AI compliance awareness training within 12 months of this procedure's adoption, including:

- Awareness of the IPC-OHRC Joint Principles
- Awareness of MFIPPA Bill 97 amendments
- Awareness of this whistleblower procedure
- Awareness of the recourse workflow (06-recourse-workflow.md)

Training is delivered through existing City L&D channels; Sentainel Municipal Edition includes a 30-minute online training module that satisfies this requirement.
