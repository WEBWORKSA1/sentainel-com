## What makes NOTL different

Three distinguishing factors:

**Factor 1: Tripled regulatory scope.** Town + Hydro + Library = three MFIPPA institutions, each with distinct privacy expectations (the Library has heightened common-law confidentiality; Hydro has Ontario Energy Board overlay; Town has standard MFIPPA). A single PIA across institutions is more efficient than three separate PIAs but requires explicit institutional sign-offs.

**Factor 2: International heritage-tourism reputational stakes.** NOTL receives Shaw Festival, wine country, and heritage tourism visitors from across Canada, the US, the EU, and Asia. A privacy incident affecting tourists generates international media coverage disproportionate to the Town's resident population.

**Factor 3: Regional best-practice opportunity.** No Ontario municipality has yet published a cross-institutional MFIPPA Bill 97 framework. NOTL has the cleanest opportunity to be first — and to become the citation reference for the dozens of other Ontario shared-service public-sector arrangements that will eventually need this.
