## Cross-Institutional Edition

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance for the Town of Niagara-on-the-Lake**
**Date: May 2026 · Confidential when activated**

---

## Purpose

This document is NOTL's standing playbook for responding to an IPC investigation about AI use across the Town, Library, or Hydro shared environment.

The cross-institutional context means that a single IPC complaint may implicate all three institutions, and response coordination is essential.

---

## Trigger conditions

Activate when any of the following occur:

1. Any of the three institutions receives written notice from the IPC of an investigation, complaint, or inquiry under MFIPPA
2. A media outlet contacts any institution about AI use and refers to IPC scrutiny
3. The cross-institutional AI Governance Lead becomes aware of an AI-related complaint likely to be escalated to the IPC
4. A Library or Hydro-specific complaint reaches the Library Board or Hydro leadership

---

## Immediate actions (Days 0–3)

### Day 0

1. **Notify the cross-institutional AI Governance Lead** + institutional sub-leads
2. **Notify CAO + Library Board chair + Hydro leadership** as appropriate based on the complaint scope
3. **Convene cross-institutional response team:**
 - AI Governance Lead (coordinator)
 - Institutional sub-lead(s) for affected institution(s)
 - Fred Cervantes (IT Manager)
 - Clerk
 - External counsel (engage if not already retained)
4. **Preserve all relevant records** — across all three institutions if the shared IT environment is implicated
5. **Issue litigation hold** to relevant staff at all affected institutions

### Day 1

6. **Acknowledge IPC communication** within 24 hours
7. **Identify the IPC investigator** and obtain contact information
8. **Assess complaint scope:**
 - Which institution(s) is implicated
 - Which AI system or decision is at issue
 - Whether the complaint touches the shared IT environment broadly
 - Which IPC-OHRC principles are implicated
 - Which MFIPPA Bill 97 obligations are implicated

### Day 2–3

9. **Prepare initial evidence packet** drawing from this bundle's documents specific to the implicated institution(s)
10. **Engage counsel** for review before submission
11. **Identify privilege concerns** including any cross-institutional documents (the MOU may be subject to internal-deliberation privilege)

## Cross-institutional considerations

### When the complaint implicates multiple institutions

A single IPC complaint can implicate all three institutions if the underlying AI use is in the shared IT environment (e.g., M365 Copilot processing data that crosses institutional boundaries). In such cases:

- The cross-institutional AI Governance Lead is the primary point of contact for the IPC
- Institutional sub-leads coordinate institution-specific responses
- Joint responses are submitted where the IPC's questions span institutions
- Institution-specific responses are submitted where the IPC's questions are scoped to one institution

### When the complaint is institution-specific

If the complaint is clearly scoped to one institution (e.g., Library borrower data, Hydro smart meter data), the institutional sub-lead takes primary responsibility with the AI Governance Lead in coordinating capacity.

### Information-sharing protocols

The Cross-Institutional MOU (described in 03-aia-template.md § 3.2) governs information sharing during an IPC investigation. Specifically:

- The AI Governance Lead has access to all relevant records across the three institutions for investigation purposes
- Institutional sub-leads have institution-specific record access
- Library borrower information is shared only with the Library sub-lead and the AI Governance Lead; not with Town or Hydro staff
- Hydro customer information is similarly compartmentalized

## Investigation phase

See Thorold 07-ipc-complaint-prep.md § "Investigation phase" for standard IPC pattern. NOTL-specific considerations:

**Site visit coordination:** If the IPC requests a site visit, coordinate with all three institutions to facilitate access while preserving institutional autonomy.

**Tourism + visitor data considerations:** If the complaint relates to visitor or tourist data (especially international visitors), additional jurisdictions may have parallel interest (Quebec, US state regulators, EU GDPR authorities). External counsel should advise on multi-jurisdiction implications.

**Library borrower confidentiality:** Library borrower records are particularly sensitive even within the institutional response. The Library sub-lead may invoke common-law librarian-borrower confidentiality in addition to MFIPPA protections.

## Mediation track

IPC mediation outcomes typically result in the Town agreeing to remediation that this bundle has already prepared. Specifically:

- AI Inventory publication (template at 01-ai-inventory.md)
- PIA + AIA completion (templates at 02 + 03)
- Recourse mechanism standup (template at 06)
- Cross-Institutional MOU adoption (described in 03)

NOTL's existing Sentainel artifacts at the time of mediation will satisfy 80-90% of typical mediation outcomes.

---

## Formal order phase

See Thorold 07-ipc-complaint-prep.md § "Formal order phase" for standard pattern. NOTL-specific considerations:

A formal IPC order against any one of the three institutions may have cascading effects on the other two through the shared IT environment. Specifically:

- An order directing Town to cease an AI use may require the Library and Hydro to also cease use of the same shared system
- An order to remediate the shared IT environment affects all three institutions
- The Cross-Institutional MOU should address how formal orders against one institution propagate to the others

## Communications strategy

### Internal

Brief Town council, Library Board, and Hydro leadership in closed session within 5 business days of the trigger event.

Brief department heads at all three institutions on the scope.

### External

Media: designate the Mayor or designate as primary media contact for Town-related coverage; Library Board chair for Library-related; Hydro CEO for Hydro-related. The AI Governance Lead is the technical media contact across institutions.

Public: post a brief statement on notl.com within 5 business days if the matter is publicly known. Mirror on Library and Hydro sites as appropriate.

## Pre-positioned evidence checklist

For a successful IPC defense, the following should exist when a complaint arrives:

- [ ] Cross-institutional Public AI Inventory on all three institutional websites
- [ ] Designated cross-institutional AI Governance Lead + institutional sub-leads (named publicly)
- [ ] Adopted Cross-Institutional AI Compliance MOU
- [ ] Adopted Generative AI Staff Use Policy at all three institutions
- [ ] Published recourse mechanism with cross-institutional routing
- [ ] Quarterly status reports to council + Library Board + Hydro leadership
- [ ] Vendor compliance attestations for shared IT environment vendors (especially Microsoft, HSO if engaged)
- [ ] NIST AI RMF mapping for each significant AI exposure across all three institutions
- [ ] Library-specific borrower confidentiality assessment
- [ ] Hydro-specific OEB compliance assessment

NOTL currently has none of these in public-record form.
