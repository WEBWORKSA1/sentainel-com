## How to read this scorecard

This document scores the Town's current public-record AI compliance posture against the 7 IPC-OHRC Joint Principles, system-by-system. Scores:

- **✅ SATISFIED** — evidence on public record demonstrates alignment
- **🟡 PARTIAL** — some evidence exists, gaps remain
- **🔴 GAP** — no public evidence; obligation not addressed
- **N/A** — principle does not meaningfully apply to this system

A defensible posture under IPC + OHRC scrutiny requires at minimum 🟡 PARTIAL on all applicable cells. Currently, **48 of 49 cells are 🔴 GAP** — typical for Ontario municipalities in May 2026.

## Per-principle commentary

### p1 Valid and Reliable
No accuracy testing or evaluation framework exists for any AI exposure. **Action:** Establish accuracy testing requirements in the AI Governance Lead's mandate.

### p2 Safe
Decommissioning authority not designated. The 2024–2025 astroturfing incident is unaddressed in policy. **Action:** Adopt Council Astroturfing Response Procedure (in 03-aia-template.md § 5). Designate decommissioning authority for any future production AI.

### p3 Privacy Protective
No privacy-by-design statement on AI exposures. Staff GenAI policy gap creates MFIPPA risk. **Action:** Adopt Generative AI Staff Use Policy (in 03-aia-template.md § 6).

### p4 Human Rights Affirming
No disparate-impact analysis for NRPS ALPR within Thorold. Cultural community concentrations (Welsh, Italian, Filipino) not specifically considered. **Action:** Coordinate with NRPS on joint disparate-impact analysis covering all four cities including Thorold-specific cuts.

### p5 Transparent
No public AI inventory. The 2024–2025 incident not publicly addressed by the Town. **Action:** Publish AI inventory on thorold.ca + retrospective public statement about the 2024–2025 campaign.

### p6 Accountable Governance
No designated AI Governance Lead at any level. **Action:** Single appointment, 30 days, covers all 7 principles' governance requirement.

### p7 Accountable Recourse
No public-facing mechanism for residents to challenge AI-influenced decisions or flag suspected synthetic communication. **Action:** Adopt 06-recourse-workflow.md.

## The single most consequential change

If council can only authorize one action, the highest-leverage move is **designating an AI Governance Lead within Corporate Services**. This single appointment satisfies the governance requirement across all 7 principles, creates a documented accountability chain, and gives the Town one named person who can speak to IPC + OHRC if an inquiry begins.

The AI Governance Lead does not need to be a new hire. A reassignment of an existing senior staff member (Clerk, IT Director, or Director of Corporate Services) is sufficient. The role can be described in two sentences in a council resolution.
