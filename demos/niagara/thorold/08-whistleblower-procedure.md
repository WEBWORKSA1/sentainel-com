# Whistleblower Procedure — Town of Thorold
## Staff reporting of AI non-compliance per MFIPPA Bill 97 + IPC-OHRC p7

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance · Implementation-ready procedure**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## Why this exists

MFIPPA Bill 97 (effective January 1, 2027) includes whistleblower protection for staff who report MFIPPA violations.

IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 (Accountable):

> "Members of institutions should be empowered through safe whistleblowing protections to report instances where an AI system does not comply with legal, technical, or policy requirements. Whistleblowers should be able to report non-compliance to an independent oversight body responsible for reviewing or overseeing the AI system, without fear of reprisal."

The Town of Thorold currently has general workplace whistleblower channels but no AI-specific channel. This procedure fills the gap.

---

## Scope

This procedure covers staff reports of:

1. **AI system non-compliance** with MFIPPA, IPC-OHRC Principles, or Town AI policy
2. **Undisclosed AI use** in Town communications or decisions
3. **Discriminatory AI outputs** observed in operational use
4. **Privacy breach risks** from AI systems
5. **Vendor non-compliance** with Town AI vendor due diligence requirements
6. **Council Astroturfing Procedure failures** (Thorold-specific category) — staff observation of the procedure not being followed

---

## Reporting channels

Three channels, in increasing protection:

### Channel 1: Internal direct report

Staff can report to:

- Their direct manager
- The AI Governance Lead
- The Town Clerk
- The CAO

This is the default channel for routine concerns. The Town protects against reprisal under standard Ontario employment law + Town HR policies.

### Channel 2: AI Governance Lead anonymous intake

Staff can submit anonymously via:

- A dedicated email inbox (`ai-whistleblower@thorold.com` or similar) accessible only to the AI Governance Lead
- A physical drop-box at Town Hall (anonymous paper reports)
- An anonymous web form on the Town intranet

The AI Governance Lead is required to investigate within timelines defined below.

### Channel 3: External oversight body referral

For severe concerns or where Channels 1–2 have failed, staff are explicitly permitted to report to:

- The Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario (IPC)
- The Ontario Human Rights Commission (OHRC)
- The Ontario Ombudsman

The Town acknowledges and protects this right. **No employment consequence shall result from a staff member's external reporting** consistent with MFIPPA Bill 97 whistleblower protections.

---

## Investigation timelines

| Action | Channel 1 | Channel 2 | Channel 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acknowledge receipt | Same day | 3 business days | (IPC/OHRC manages) |
| Initial review | 5 business days | 10 business days | (IPC/OHRC manages) |
| Determination | 30 business days | 60 business days | (IPC/OHRC manages) |
| Action taken | Communicated to reporter (if known) | Communicated via anonymous channel response | (IPC/OHRC manages) |

---

## Documentation requirements

For every report (excluding identifying information of anonymous reporters), the AI Governance Lead maintains:

- Report timestamp + unique ID
- Category of concern
- AI system or procedure referenced
- Investigation summary
- Determination (substantiated / unsubstantiated / referred)
- Action taken
- Date of resolution

This log is required evidence in IPC investigations under MFIPPA Bill 97. Aggregate statistics are included in the quarterly public AI compliance report.

---

## Anti-reprisal protection

The Town of Thorold commits in writing that **no staff member will face disciplinary, employment, or assignment consequences as a result of a good-faith report under this procedure**.

This includes:

- No reduction in compensation, benefits, or status
- No transfer or reassignment intended as reprisal
- No exclusion from training, advancement, or projects
- No adverse reference or recommendation
- No disclosure of identity of anonymous reporters
- No exclusion from collective bargaining protections (where applicable)

Reprisal allegations are themselves whistleblower reports subject to this procedure.

---

## Confidentiality protection

The AI Governance Lead and any investigators handle whistleblower reports under strict confidentiality. Internal communication about specific reports is limited to a need-to-know basis. Identification of anonymous reporters is prohibited even where investigators believe they could deduce identity.

Where a report involves criminal conduct (extremely unlikely in the AI compliance context but possible), the AI Governance Lead consults with Town legal counsel before any disclosure to law enforcement, with priority on preserving whistleblower confidentiality.

---

## Training requirement

All Town staff receive annual training that includes:

- The existence of this procedure
- The three reporting channels
- The anti-reprisal protection
- The categories of reportable concerns
- The 2024–2025 Thorold astroturfing incident as a case-study reason for procedural integrity

For a Town of Thorold's scale, training can be delivered as a 30-minute video or in-person session during regular staff training cycles. Cost: minimal.

---

## Interaction with collective agreements + employment law

This procedure operates in addition to, not in replacement of, existing protections under:

- Ontario Employment Standards Act
- Applicable collective bargaining agreements
- Public Service Act (where applicable)
- Common law wrongful dismissal protections

The procedure cannot reduce these protections. It supplements them with AI-specific reporting channels.

## Approval pathway

This procedure should be:

1. Reviewed by Town legal counsel for collective agreement + employment law compatibility
2. Reviewed by Town HR
3. Adopted by Council resolution (the public adoption itself is part of whistleblower protection — staff know the procedure exists)
4. Published on thorold.com and the Town intranet
5. Distributed to all staff with sign-off acknowledgment

Recommended adoption timeline: 90 days from the date of AI Governance Lead designation.
