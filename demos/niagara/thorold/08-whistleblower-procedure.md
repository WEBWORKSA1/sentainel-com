# Town of Thorold — Whistleblower Procedure for AI Non-Compliance
## MFIPPA Bill 97 + IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 implementation

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance for the Town of Thorold**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## Purpose

MFIPPA Bill 97 introduces whistleblower protection (effective January 1, 2027) for municipal employees who report breaches of MFIPPA. IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 requires that "members of institutions should be empowered through safe whistleblowing protections to report instances where an AI system does not comply with legal, technical, or policy requirements."

The Town of Thorold does not currently have a publicly documented whistleblower procedure specific to AI non-compliance. This document is the Town's procedure template.

Thorold-specific consideration: in a small municipality, whistleblower protection is particularly important because the institutional anonymity that protects whistleblowers in larger organizations does not exist. A whistleblower at a 22,000-resident town is identifiable. The procedure must therefore be specifically designed to protect against retaliation in a small-team environment.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to staff or contractors of:

- The Town of Thorold (all departments)
- The Town's contracted service providers in any role that touches AI processing of municipal information

It covers concerns about:

- AI systems used by the Town that may not comply with MFIPPA, MFIPPA Bill 97, IPC-OHRC Principles, the Trustworthy AI Framework, or Town policy
- Staff misuse of AI tools (e.g., prohibited entry of MFIPPA-protected information into public AI services)
- Vendor AI products that may not comply with applicable requirements
- AI-related practices that may infringe on Code-protected rights
- AI-related incidents (false positives, biased outputs, security exposures) that are not being addressed by responsible managers

---

## Reporting channels

The procedure provides **three reporting channels** with different levels of confidentiality:

### Channel 1 — Internal: AI Governance Lead

**When to use:** First-line concerns where the AI Governance Lead is not the subject of the concern

**How to use:**
- Email ai-governance-lead@thorold.ca
- In-person meeting at request
- Written report to AI Governance Lead's office

**Confidentiality:** Communications to the AI Governance Lead are subject to the same confidentiality protections as MFIPPA Privacy Officer communications. The AI Governance Lead may share the substance of the concern with the CAO and external counsel for response purposes, but the reporter's identity is protected unless disclosure is necessary for the response.

### Channel 2 — Internal: CAO

**When to use:** Concerns about the AI Governance Lead, or concerns the reporter prefers to escalate above the AI Governance Lead

**How to use:**
- Email cao@thorold.ca with subject line "Confidential: AI Compliance Concern"
- In-person meeting at request

**Confidentiality:** Same as Channel 1.

### Channel 3 — External: IPC + OHRC

**When to use:** Concerns that have not been adequately addressed internally, or concerns the reporter prefers to take directly to an external regulator

**How to use:**
- IPC complaint at ipc.on.ca/complaint
- OHRC complaint at hrto.ca (if Code-protected ground is implicated)

**Confidentiality:** The IPC and OHRC have their own confidentiality protections for whistleblowers, governed by their respective statutes.

---

## Anti-retaliation commitment

The Town of Thorold commits that no staff member who reports an AI compliance concern in good faith through any of the above channels will face:

- Termination, demotion, suspension, or denial of promotion
- Reduction in pay or benefits
- Reassignment without consent to less favorable duties or location
- Disclosure of identity beyond what is necessary to respond to the concern
- Informal retaliation including social ostracism within the workplace, exclusion from meetings, or unjustified performance criticism
- Negative reference checks or post-employment retaliation

This commitment is binding on all Town management and is enforceable by the AI Governance Lead, the CAO, and external counsel.

---

## Small-municipality protections

Because Thorold's small size makes whistleblower anonymity difficult to maintain in practice, the procedure includes:

1. **External counsel intake option.** The Town may engage external counsel to receive whistleblower reports if the reporter prefers not to be identified internally. External counsel reports the substance of the concern to the Town without identifying the reporter unless the reporter consents.

2. **Cross-municipality referral option.** The Town may, with the reporter's consent, refer the concern to the AI Governance Lead of a peer Niagara municipality (Niagara Falls, St. Catharines, or NOTL) if the reporter believes local Town leadership is the subject of the concern.

3. **Time-displaced response.** Where possible, the Town's response to a whistleblower concern is structured to avoid temporal correlation with the report (e.g., a policy change made several months after a report is less likely to identify the reporter than an immediate change).

4. **No-reprisal certification at hiring + annual.** All Town staff certify upon hiring and annually that they understand the no-reprisal commitment and the procedure.

## Response procedure

### Step 1: Receipt

Within 5 business days of receiving a whistleblower report through any channel, the recipient:
- Acknowledges receipt to the reporter
- Records the concern in a confidential log
- Assesses whether the concern requires immediate action (e.g., suspending an AI system pending investigation)

### Step 2: Investigation

The AI Governance Lead, or designated investigator if the AI Governance Lead is the subject of the concern, investigates the matter. Investigation may include:
- Review of relevant records (AI system logs, vendor contracts, decisions affected)
- Interviews with staff involved (without disclosing the reporter's identity)
- External counsel consultation
- Technical review by IT Director

### Step 3: Findings

The investigator prepares a written findings document including:
- Description of the concern (without identifying details about the reporter)
- Investigative steps taken
- Findings of fact
- Conclusion (concern substantiated, partially substantiated, not substantiated)
- Recommended remediation

### Step 4: Action

The CAO or designated authority directs remediation action based on the findings. Action may include:
- Suspension of an AI system pending remediation
- Update to a policy or procedure
- Vendor escalation
- Staff training or discipline (where misconduct is found)
- External reporting to the IPC, OHRC, or other authority if required

### Step 5: Reporter feedback

Within 60 days of the report (or longer if investigation is complex), the AI Governance Lead provides the reporter with:
- A summary of the investigation findings
- The action taken
- An explanation of why the matter is considered closed, or what next steps are planned

### Step 6: Annual public reporting

The AI Governance Lead publishes an annual summary of whistleblower reports (aggregate, no identifying details) in the same annual report as the recourse mechanism (06-recourse-workflow.md).

---

## Documentation

The AI Governance Lead maintains a confidential log of all whistleblower reports including:
- Date received
- Channel used
- Substance of concern (anonymized as needed)
- Investigation steps
- Findings
- Remediation action
- Reporter feedback
- Reporter feedback received (yes/no)

This log is subject to MFIPPA protections and is not publicly disclosed. The aggregate annual report is the public-facing component.
