# Town of Thorold — Public-Facing AI Recourse Workflow
## IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 (Accountable Recourse) Implementation

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance for the Town of Thorold**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## Purpose

IPC-OHRC Joint Principle 7 requires public-sector institutions to "establish a mechanism to receive and respond to privacy, transparency, or human rights questions or concerns... or to any challenges concerning how the AI system arrived at a decision or was used during a decision-making process."

Thorold currently has no such mechanism specific to AI use. This document is a publication-ready template for the Town's AI Recourse Workflow.

Thorold-specific consideration: this workflow also incorporates a **"Suspected Synthetic Communication Report"** channel that does not appear in standard municipal recourse procedures. This is the residents' counterpart to the Council Astroturfing Response Procedure documented in `03-aia-template.md` § 5.

---

## Public-facing page (to be hosted on thorold.ca)

### **"Concerns About the Town's Use of AI"**

The Town of Thorold is committed to responsible use of artificial intelligence. If you have concerns about how the Town has used AI in a decision that affects you, this page explains your options.

---

### When this page applies

This recourse workflow applies if you believe:

- The Town used AI in a decision that affected you (e.g., a bylaw enforcement action, a permit decision, a service-delivery decision)
- The Town used AI to generate or summarize information that was provided to you and you have concerns about its accuracy
- The Town's website chatbot or other public-facing AI system provided you with information that was incorrect or that you found problematic
- You suspect that a wave of communications to council on an issue you care about may have been AI-generated rather than authentic resident input
- You believe the Town's use of AI may discriminate against you on a Code-protected ground

This workflow does not replace the Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act formal access and correction processes. If you want to formally access records or request correction of personal information about you, please use the FOI process at thorold.ca/foi.

---

### How to file a concern

**Step 1:** Send an email to ai-concerns@thorold.ca (or mail to the Town Clerk, Town of Thorold, 3540 Schmon Parkway, Thorold ON L2V 4A9) describing:

- The decision, communication, or AI use that concerns you
- The date or approximate date of the event
- Your contact information (so we can respond)
- The outcome you are seeking (correction, explanation, decision review)

**Step 2:** The Town's AI Governance Lead acknowledges receipt within 5 business days.

**Step 3:** Within 30 days, the AI Governance Lead provides a written response that:

- Confirms or denies that AI was used in the decision or communication
- Explains what role AI played, if any
- Identifies the responsible Town staff member
- States what action, if any, the Town is taking in response
- Informs you of your right to escalate to the Ontario Information and Privacy Commissioner if you are not satisfied

**Step 4:** If you are not satisfied with the Town's response, you can:

- File a complaint with the IPC at ipc.on.ca/complaint
- File a Human Rights Code complaint with the Ontario Human Rights Tribunal at hrto.ca

---

### Specifically for suspected synthetic communications

If you suspect that a wave of communications to council on an issue may have been AI-generated rather than authentic resident input, please use the form at thorold.ca/synthetic-communication-report.

The form asks:

- The council issue or vote you are concerned about
- The reasons for your concern (multiple identical emails, out-of-jurisdiction senders, etc.)
- Any evidence you have access to (email screenshots, sender pattern observations, etc.)

The Town takes these reports seriously. In 2024–2025, the Town's council was the target of a coordinated AI-generated email campaign on a climate policy issue, which is now publicly documented. The Town has adopted procedures to detect and respond to similar campaigns in the future.

We will not disclose your identity as a reporter without your written consent.

---

### Privacy of recourse submissions

Information you submit to the recourse mechanism is collected under MFIPPA s. 28 and is used solely to investigate and respond to your concern. We do not use this information for any other purpose without your consent.

If you would like a copy of your submission or want to correct information you previously submitted, contact the Town Clerk.

---

### Public reporting

The Town's AI Governance Lead publishes an annual summary of recourse submissions received, including:

- Number of submissions by category
- Number of submissions resulting in changes to AI use
- Number of submissions escalated to the IPC or OHRC
- Trends and lessons learned

This report is published on thorold.ca and presented to council at the first council meeting of each calendar year.

Note: Individual submissions are not disclosed in the annual summary.

## Internal procedures (not for public publication)

### Routing

- ai-concerns@thorold.ca forwards to AI Governance Lead inbox + Clerk's office
- thorold.ca/synthetic-communication-report is monitored by the Clerk's office

### Service-level commitments

- Acknowledgement within 5 business days
- Written response within 30 days
- Escalation to council if the AI Governance Lead determines the concern raises systemic or policy-level issues

### Documentation

- All submissions logged in the Town's case management system with appropriate confidentiality controls
- Annual summary prepared in November for January council presentation

### Coordination

- If submission involves NRPS-operated AI (e.g., ALPR), forward to NRPS Privacy Officer with copy to the Town
- If submission involves third-party vendor AI, coordinate with vendor's compliance contact (questionnaire at 09-vendor-questionnaire.md)
