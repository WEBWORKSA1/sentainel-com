## The 2024–2025 incident

I do not raise this lightly. In 2024–2025, a coordinated AI-generated email campaign targeted Thorold Council. Per Canada's National Observer (April 2026), and earlier reporting in CBC and The Pointer, a climate denial group used customized chatbots to generate mass emails opposing climate policy. The campaign succeeded: Thorold Council voted 7-1 to withdraw from the Partners for Climate Protection program.

This is **the only documented case in this four-city bundle of AI being used as a vector of harm against a Niagara municipal institution**. Niagara Falls, St. Catharines, and Niagara-on-the-Lake have AI compliance exposures. Only Thorold has a recorded AI incident affecting council legitimacy.

I want to be precise about what I am saying and not saying:

- I am **not** suggesting Thorold Council made a wrong vote
- I am **not** assigning fault to council members who acted in good faith on what they understood to be constituent communications
- I am **not** suggesting this is unique to Thorold — the same attack pattern has hit Hamilton and may hit any council in Ontario

What I am saying is: **Council members were the direct targets and direct victims of an AI-driven harm**. And the defensive infrastructure that would have helped — synthetic communication detection, council procedural integrity protocols, public recourse mechanisms — did not exist at the Town in standardized form, and still does not.

This is fixable. And this is the part of the audit bundle where the IPC, the OHRC, and emerging Ontario AI governance standards have the most operational guidance to offer.
