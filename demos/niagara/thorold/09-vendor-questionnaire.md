# Town of Thorold — AI Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire
## For procurement of AI-containing products and services

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance for the Town of Thorold**
**Date: May 2026**

---

## How to use this questionnaire

When the Town is considering procurement of any product or service that includes AI functionality, this questionnaire is included in the RFP or RFQ documents. Vendors complete the questionnaire as part of their bid response.

The questionnaire is also retroactively applicable to existing vendor relationships. The AI Governance Lead may send this questionnaire to current vendors of products that include AI features.

Thorold-specific consideration: the Town's procurement budget is smaller than its peer cities, which means vendor leverage is limited. The questionnaire is designed to be answerable by serious vendors without requiring extensive customization, while still gathering enough information to support a credible PIA and AIA.

---

## Section A — Basic AI disclosure

A1. Does your product or service include any artificial intelligence, machine learning, automated decision-making, or generative AI features? *(Yes / No / Partial / Don't know)*

A2. If yes, please describe the AI features at a level a non-technical reader can understand. *(Free text)*

A3. Are the AI features built by your organization, licensed from a third party, or both? *(Specify)*

A4. For licensed third-party AI components, please name the providers. *(Examples: OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft Azure AI, Google Vertex AI, AWS Bedrock, others)*

A5. For each AI feature, is it on by default for new customers, off by default, or configurable per customer? *(Specify per feature)*

---

## Section B — Personal information processing

B1. Will the AI feature process personal information about residents, staff, or visitors of the Town? *(Yes / No / Possibly)*

B2. What categories of personal information will be processed? *(Names, contact information, location, health information, financial information, demographic information, other — specify)*

B3. Will personal information be used to train, fine-tune, or improve any AI model? *(Yes / No / Optional with customer opt-out / Unknown)*

B4. If personal information may be used for training, please provide the contract terms or opt-out procedure. *(Provide text or reference)*

B5. Where geographically is personal information processed? Please list specific countries and data centre regions for each AI feature. *(Critical question)*

B6. If processing is outside Canada, please describe the cross-border transfer safeguards. *(MFIPPA implications)*

B7. How long is personal information retained for AI processing purposes? *(Provide retention period for each data category)*

B8. Can the Town request deletion of personal information processed by the AI feature? If yes, how? *(Required for MFIPPA Bill 97)*

---

## Section C — Accuracy and reliability

C1. Please provide accuracy or performance metrics for the AI feature. *(Specific numbers, not generalities)*

C2. How are accuracy metrics measured and how often are they updated? *(Frequency + methodology)*

C3. Have you conducted bias or fairness testing on the AI feature? If yes, please provide the methodology and results. *(IPC-OHRC p4)*

C4. Does the AI feature have known failure modes the Town should be aware of? *(Free text)*

C5. What happens if the AI feature returns an incorrect or potentially harmful result? *(Error handling, fallback, escalation)*

---

## Section D — Transparency and explainability

D1. Does the AI feature provide explanations for its outputs to end users? *(Yes / No / Partial)*

D2. Can the Town request technical documentation of the AI feature's design and operation? *(IPC-OHRC p5 traceability)*

D3. Will end users be informed when they are interacting with AI rather than a human? *(IPC-OHRC p5 visible requirement)*

D4. What user-facing labelling or disclosure is provided to indicate AI involvement? *(Specify exact language or screenshots)*

---

## Section E — Governance and accountability

E1. Who at your organization is accountable for the AI feature's compliance with applicable regulations? *(Named role and contact)*

E2. Do you have an AI governance framework that includes documented policies, designated AI roles, and ongoing oversight? *(Yes / No — provide overview if yes)*

E3. Are you compliant with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework? Please describe alignment specifics. *(Critical question — supports Town's safe-harbor matrix)*

E4. Are you compliant with applicable Ontario regulations including MFIPPA, MFIPPA Bill 97 (effective July 1, 2026), IPC-OHRC Joint Principles, and (when proclaimed) EDSTA Bill 194? *(Yes / Partial / Working toward / No)*

E5. Will you support the Town's PIA and AIA processes including providing information needed for these assessments? *(Required)*

---

## Section F — Recourse and incident response

F1. What is your incident response procedure for AI-related incidents (false positives, biased outputs, security exposures)? *(Provide procedure or reference)*

F2. How quickly will you notify the Town if you become aware of an AI-related issue affecting the Town's data or users? *(Specify notification timeline)*

F3. Do you provide a mechanism for end users to challenge AI outputs or seek correction? *(Yes / No — describe)*

F4. Will you cooperate with the Town's recourse workflow (described at 06-recourse-workflow.md in the Town's compliance bundle)? *(Yes / No / Conditional)*

F5. Will you cooperate with an IPC investigation involving the Town's use of your AI feature? *(Yes / No / Conditional)*

---

## Section G — Subprocessors and supply chain

G1. List all subprocessors that may have access to the Town's data or to data processed by the AI feature on behalf of the Town. *(Required)*

G2. What is your process for notifying the Town of new subprocessors? *(Notification timeline + opt-out option if any)*

G3. For each subprocessor, what category of data do they have access to and where do they process it? *(Country + data centre region)*

G4. Do all subprocessors meet the same compliance requirements you commit to in this questionnaire? *(Yes / No / Variable)*

---

## Section H — Security

H1. What security certifications do you hold? *(SOC 2, ISO 27001, FedRAMP, etc.)*

H2. How is data encrypted at rest and in transit? *(Encryption specifications)*

H3. What is your incident notification timeline for security breaches affecting the Town? *(Required for MFIPPA breach reporting)*

H4. Do you carry cyber liability insurance? At what coverage level? *(Critical for vendor due diligence)*

---

## Section I — Contract terms

I1. Will you sign a Data Processing Agreement compliant with MFIPPA Bill 97 requirements? *(Yes / No / Conditional)*

I2. Will you accept contract terms that include the Town's right to audit AI-related compliance? *(Yes / No / Conditional)*

I3. Will you accept contract terms that allow the Town to suspend the AI feature in response to compliance concerns without penalty? *(Yes / No / Conditional)*

I4. What is your standard contract term for vendor AI features? Are shorter or no-fixed-term arrangements available? *(Critical for small municipalities with limited budget flexibility)*

---

## Section J — Pricing and economics

J1. What is the pricing structure for the AI feature? *(Per user, per transaction, fixed, hybrid)*

J2. Are there usage caps or surge charges the Town should be aware of? *(Important for small budgets)*

J3. Are there opt-outs for specific AI features that reduce cost? *(Helps the Town manage AI exposure)*

J4. Do you offer pricing accommodations for small Ontario municipalities (population <30,000)? *(Important for Thorold specifically)*

---

## Scoring rubric

Responses are scored on a 3-point scale:

- **3 (Strong):** Direct, specific, supportive answer
- **2 (Acceptable):** Partial or conditional answer that does not raise concerns
- **1 (Concern):** Answer that raises compliance, security, or operational concerns

A vendor scoring 3 on all critical questions (B5, B6, B7, C3, D3, E3, E4, F5, I1, I2) is preferred. A vendor scoring 1 on any critical question requires AI Governance Lead review before procurement may proceed.

---

## Notes for procurement officer

This questionnaire is a starting point, not a fixed contract document. Vendors may propose alternative language for specific questions. The AI Governance Lead reviews proposed alternatives and approves or requires renegotiation.

For existing vendor relationships, the questionnaire is sent with a 30-day response deadline and a clear statement that the questionnaire is for compliance documentation purposes, not contract renegotiation. Most established vendors will respond cooperatively because the answers also support their other municipal customer relationships.

If a vendor refuses to complete the questionnaire or returns evasive responses, this is itself information the Town should document. Persistent vendor non-cooperation may justify procurement reconsideration at contract renewal.
