# Whistleblower Procedure — City of St. Catharines
## Staff Reporting of AI Non-Compliance Including Catharine-Specific Concerns

**Prepared by Sentainel Compliance · May 2026**

---

## Why this exists

MFIPPA Bill 97 (effective January 1, 2027) includes whistleblower protection. IPC-OHRC p7 requires safe whistleblowing channels.

For St. Catharines specifically, the procedure has heightened importance because **Catharine is in active production** and staff in Customer Service, IT, and Communications interact with the system daily. Staff are the first to observe issues before they become public-facing problems.

---

## Scope

This procedure covers staff reports of:

1. AI system non-compliance with MFIPPA, IPC-OHRC, or City AI policy
2. Undisclosed AI use in City communications or decisions
3. Discriminatory AI outputs observed in operational use
4. Privacy breach risks from AI systems
5. Vendor non-compliance (HSO, Microsoft, or other AI vendors)
6. **Catharine-specific concerns** (St. Catharines-specific category):
 - Catharine providing inappropriate or harmful responses
 - Catharine routing emergency-critical inquiries incorrectly
 - Catharine handling personal information outside disclosed parameters
 - HSO or Microsoft conduct inconsistent with the DPA
 - Catharine training data including non-consented information
 - Catharine planned extensions (IT support, Teams, phone) deployed without proper AIA

---

## Reporting channels

### Channel 1: Internal direct report

Staff can report to:

- Their direct manager
- Jonathan Wright (Manager of Customer Service, Catharine operational lead)
- The AI Governance Lead (once formally designated)
- The City Clerk
- The CAO

### Channel 2: AI Governance Lead anonymous intake

- Dedicated email: `ai-whistleblower@stcatharines.ca`
- Physical drop-box at City Hall
- Anonymous web form on City intranet

### Channel 3: External oversight body referral

- Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario (IPC)
- Ontario Human Rights Commission (OHRC)
- Ontario Ombudsman
- For Catharine-specific vendor concerns: Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (where Microsoft Canada operations may also be implicated)

The City explicitly protects staff right to external reporting under MFIPPA Bill 92 + Bill 97.

---

## Catharine-specific staff considerations

Staff in Customer Service interacting with Catharine daily may observe:

- Patterns of Catharine failure on specific topics
- Specific resident demographics receiving worse Catharine responses
- Workarounds being developed informally to compensate for Catharine limitations
- Unauthorized expansion of Catharine into decision-support contexts beyond its design
- HSO or Microsoft staff accessing City data outside DPA scope

Each of these is a reportable concern under Category 6. **Staff observing these patterns are the City's first defense against systemic Catharine issues becoming IPC complaints.**

The AI Governance Lead has a specific responsibility to investigate Category 6 reports with high priority and to engage HSO + Microsoft vendor escalation pathways as appropriate.

---

## Investigation timelines

Standard timelines (see other city procedures) plus:

- **Category 6 (Catharine-specific) Channel 1 reports:** initial review 3 business days, urgent if pattern suggests systemic issue
- **Category 6 vendor-related reports:** vendor notification within 5 business days if substantiated

---

## Documentation requirements

Standard plus:

- Vendor (HSO / Microsoft) notification record for Category 6 reports
- Catharine pattern analysis (5+ similar reports may indicate systemic issue)
- Linkage to recourse workflow records where Catharine output complained of by residents corresponds to staff observations

---

## Anti-reprisal protection

The City of St. Catharines commits in writing that **no staff member will face disciplinary, employment, or assignment consequences as a result of a good-faith report under this procedure**.

This includes specifically:

- No reduction in Customer Service responsibilities for staff who report Catharine issues
- No reassignment to non-Catharine duties as reprisal
- No exclusion from Catharine extension projects as reprisal
- No adverse effect on relationship with HSO or Microsoft personnel

Reprisal allegations are themselves whistleblower reports.

---

## Confidentiality protection

Standard confidentiality (see other city procedures) plus:

- Catharine-specific reports may identify specific resident interactions or specific Customer Service staff. The AI Governance Lead handles these with extra care to prevent staff or resident identification.
- HSO + Microsoft involvement does not extend confidentiality firewall to vendor personnel — staff identities are protected from vendor knowledge during investigation.

---

## Training requirement

All City staff receive annual training that includes:

- This procedure
- The three reporting channels
- The anti-reprisal protection
- Reportable categories including Catharine-specific Category 6
- Specific examples of Category 6 observations and reporting pathways
- The relationship between staff observations and resident recourse workflow

**Customer Service staff specifically receive additional training** on Catharine-related observations and reporting because they are the front-line.
