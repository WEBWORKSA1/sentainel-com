## License + reuse

Papers in this directory are published openly for the benefit of Ontario municipalities and the broader public-sector AI governance community. They may be adapted, excerpted, and incorporated into municipal policy work with attribution to the Sentainel Compliance Research Group.

## Editorial standards

Each paper goes through the following internal review before publication:

1. **Factual accuracy** — every empirical claim is sourced to public records, peer-reviewed research, or named legal instruments. Inferences are flagged.
2. **Legal framing** — regulatory references are checked against current Ontario statute and IPC/OHRC published guidance. Nothing in these papers constitutes a legal opinion; municipalities should retain qualified counsel for specific compliance questions.
3. **Procedural rigour** — proposed procedures are stress-tested against likely failure modes and adversarial scenarios.
4. **Restraint** — papers are written to be useful documents, not marketing assets. The Sentainel commercial offering is acknowledged in the author footer and nowhere else.
