# Ontario EDSTA (Bill 194) — Strategic Notes

## The "horizon obligation" framing

EDSTA's AI provisions (Schedule 1 sections 5-8) are enacted but
un-proclaimed. The statutory shell exists; the operational
content lives in Lieutenant Governor in Council regulations
under s. 7 and Minister technical standards under s. 8. Neither
set has issued as of May 2026.

Sentainel's position to customers should NOT be "you must comply
today." It should be:

> "EDSTA gives the Ontario LG the power to flip a switch with
> minimal notice. Build your accountability framework now so you
> can demonstrate readiness within days of proclamation. The
> framework you build will satisfy s. 5(3), and the same framework
> satisfies your concurrent obligations under MFIPPA Bill 97 (PIAs),
> the Code (discrimination), and PHIPA (health). One workstream,
> four shields."

## What's known vs. what's regulation-dependent

**Statutorily fixed (won't change):**
- AI definition (s. 1(1)(a)) — already aligned with NIST AI 100-1
- Scope of "public sector entity" (s. 1(1))
- Broad construction of "use" to include public, procured, and
  third-party AI (s. 1(2))
- s. 12 no-private-duty-of-care shield
- Four operational duties: information, framework, risk
  management, oversight

**Regulation-dependent (everything operational):**
- Which entities are prescribed for sections 5 and 6
- Which circumstances trigger the obligations
- Form and content of accountability frameworks
- Specific risk management steps
- Prohibited uses
- Technical standards
- Reporting cadence and recipients

## NIST AI RMF cross-walk readiness

Despite the regulatory pendency, the EDSTA framework maps cleanly
to NIST AI RMF:

| EDSTA obligation | NIST AI RMF anchor |
|---|---|
| s. 5(2) public information | MEASURE-2.8 Transparency |
| s. 5(3) accountability framework | GOVERN-1.1, GOVERN-1.4, all GOVERN |
| s. 5(4) risk management | MANAGE-1.3, MAP-5.1, MAP-5.2 |
| s. 5(5) purpose limitation | MAP-1.1, MAP-2.1 |
| s. 5(6) prohibited uses | GOVERN-1.6 (inventory + scoping) |
| s. 6(2)(a) specific-use disclosure | MEASURE-2.8, MEASURE-2.9 |
| s. 6(2)(b)(i) human oversight | MAP-3.5 |
| s. 6(2)(b)(ii) inquiry response | MEASURE-3.3, MANAGE-4.3 |

A customer implementing NIST AI RMF GOVERN-1.1 through MEASURE-3.3
is positioned to satisfy EDSTA s. 5–6 with minimal additional work
once regulations issue.

## Practical entity classification

Sentainel onboarding should classify Ontario customers along three
EDSTA-relevant axes:

1. **Direct in-scope** (will be subject when prescribed):
   - All FIPPA institutions (provincial ministries, agencies,
     hospitals via "institution" definition)
   - All MFIPPA institutions (municipalities, school boards in
     some respects, police services, public health units,
     library boards, transit commissions)
   - Children's aid societies
   - School boards (under both AI provisions and minor-info
     provisions)

2. **Indirect via vendor flow-down** (private-sector AI vendors):
   - Selling SaaS to Ontario public sector
   - Providing professional services involving AI to Ontario
     public sector
   - Embedded AI in third-party platforms sold to Ontario
     public sector
   - Procurement teams will require EDSTA-aligned terms

3. **Out of scope** (won't trigger EDSTA but may face other
   Ontario AI obligations):
   - Private-sector employers (covered by Bill 149 ESA for
     hiring AI)
   - Healthcare providers in private practice (covered by
     PHIPA for clinical AI)
   - Pure B2B AI tools with no public-sector contracts

## Cross-jurisdictional positioning

EDSTA is the first Canadian provincial AI regulatory framework.
Once proclaimed, it positions Sentainel for:

- **Federal AIDA echo**: If/when the federal Artificial
  Intelligence and Data Act passes (currently stalled), EDSTA
  experience will be transferable.

- **Quebec public-sector parallel**: CQLR c. A-2.1 (Quebec public
  sector privacy act) is the Quebec analogue. Sentainel can
  market a "public-sector AI compliance" bundle covering both.

- **Provincial spread**: BC, Alberta, Manitoba public-sector
  AI legislation expected to follow Ontario's lead. Early
  EDSTA fluency = first-mover advantage in those markets.

## Open interpretive questions for legal review

1. **"Use" boundary for ad-hoc ChatGPT access**: does an employee
   using ChatGPT to draft an email count as "use of an AI system"
   by the institution under s. 1(2)(a)? Most likely yes, but
   regulations may clarify.

2. **Third-party developed AI**: does s. 1(2)(c) capture
   off-the-shelf SaaS with embedded AI features that aren't
   the primary purpose (e.g., spell-check, search-engine
   ranking)?

3. **"Prescribed circumstances"**: which AI uses will be
   considered consequential enough to trigger s. 5 vs. s. 6?
   Most commentary expects employment decisions, benefits
   adjudication, and law enforcement uses to be in the first
   wave.

4. **s. 12 boundary**: does the no-duty-of-care shield extend
   to claims framed as breach of confidence or invasion of
   privacy where the underlying conduct violates EDSTA?

These should be flagged for the customer's outside counsel as
part of the framework development process.
