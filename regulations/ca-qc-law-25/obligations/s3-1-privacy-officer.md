required_action: |
  The person carrying on an enterprise must designate a person in
  charge of the protection of personal information. By default,
  the function is the responsibility of the person with the highest
  authority within the enterprise. The latter may delegate, in
  writing, all or part of that function to any person.

  The title and contact information of the person in charge of the
  protection of personal information must be published on the
  enterprise's website or, if the enterprise does not have a
  website, must be made available to the public by any appropriate
  means.
plain_language_summary: |
  Every Quebec enterprise must designate a privacy officer. By
  default it's the CEO. The CEO can delegate in writing — but the
  delegation must be formal and documented.

  The privacy officer's title and contact information must be
  PUBLICLY POSTED on the website. CAI compliance checks routinely
  verify this two-click accessibility.

  CRITICAL FOR AI: The privacy officer is the operational owner
  of all AI compliance activities — PIAs, breach response, consent
  management, individual rights requests, cross-border assessments.
  Without a designated and resourced officer, none of the other
  Law 25 obligations can be meaningfully fulfilled.
trigger_conditions:
