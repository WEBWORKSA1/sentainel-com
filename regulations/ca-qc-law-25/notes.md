# Quebec Law 25 — Extraction Notes

## Summary

- 15 atomic obligations extracted
- All obligations in force as of September 22, 2024
- Anonymization standard formalized May 30, 2024
- Penalty exposure: up to CAD $25M or 4% worldwide turnover
- Active CAI enforcement (444 incident reports in 2023-24)

## Why Law 25 matters strategically for Sentainel

1. **Highest North American penalty exposure.** $25M / 4% worldwide
   turnover exceeds every US state law including TRAIGA ($10K-$200K)
   and the now-replaced Colorado AI Act ($20K per violation).
   Quebec is where the dollars are.

2. **Most mature enforcement.** CAI received 444 confidentiality
   incident reports in 2023-2024 (most recent reporting period).
   Enforcement is active and increasing. No competitor jurisdiction
   in North America has this enforcement intensity.

3. **French-language barrier.** Most US competitors (TXAIMS, CO-AIMS,
   Holistic AI, Credo AI) do not extract Quebec statutes. The
   French-language and Quebec civil law context creates natural
   moat for Sentainel.

4. **Pairs strategically with Ontario.** Cross-Canada AI compliance
   requires both Quebec and Ontario coverage. Sentainel's Ontario
   stack (Bill 149 + Code + MFIPPA + EDSTA + PHIPA + Trustworthy AI)
   plus Quebec Law 25 covers ~95% of Canadian enterprise AI
   compliance needs.

5. **Extraterritorial reach drives US customer demand.** US enterprises
   with Quebec users or employees are subject to Law 25. Sentainel
   can sell to US customers based on Quebec exposure alone.

## Outstanding ambiguities for legal review

1. **Section 12.1 meaningful human involvement standard** — CAI
   casework developing; conservative reading: human must override
   AI in a non-trivial percentage of decisions
2. **Section 17 adequacy determination for US transfers** — FISA
   and CLOUD Act create ongoing uncertainty
3. **Anonymization standard for AI training data** — synthetic data
   and model memorization risks are evolving
4. **Section 14 consent granularity for evolving AI features** —
   when AI vendor adds features, is fresh consent required?
5. **Behavioral biometric scope** — whether typing patterns and
   mouse dynamics fall under biometric database notification
6. **Cross-purpose AI training under s. 12** — whether AI training
   on customer data fits any s. 12 exception

## Companion legislation tracking

- **Federal PIPEDA** — applies where commercial cross-border
  activity involved; some duplication with Law 25 but Law 25 is
  generally stricter
- **Federal CPPA/AIDA (pending)** — if passed, will create
  federal AI regulation framework; interaction with Law 25 unclear
- **Quebec Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms** — parallel
  discrimination protections; AI bias creates Charter exposure
- **Act respecting Access (CQLR c. A-2.1)** — public sector
  parallel of Law 25; covers government AI uses
- **Civil Code of Quebec art. 35-40** — base privacy framework
  underlying Law 25
- **Regulation respecting the anonymization of personal
  information (May 2024)** — formalizes anonymization standard

## Sales objection handling

The most common Quebec-related objection from US customers will be:
"We don't have Quebec offices, why do we need this?"

Response: Law 25 is extraterritorial. If you have Quebec users,
Quebec employees, Quebec investors who receive financial reports,
or Quebec resellers, you have Law 25 exposure. CAI has signaled
willingness to pursue extraterritorial cases. Penalty exposure is
$25M / 4% worldwide turnover — likely higher than your acquisition
cost.

## Notes on French/English bilingual obligations

Quebec's Charter of the French Language (Bill 101) interacts with
Law 25 in important ways:
- Consent text must be available in French
- Confidentiality policy must be available in French (s. 8.2)
- Individual rights communications must be in French if requested
- AI decisions communicated to consumers in Quebec should be
  available in French

Sentainel's French-language tooling is therefore not optional for
Quebec compliance.
