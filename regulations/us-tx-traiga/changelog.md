## Critical extraction findings

### What TRAIGA IS

- Intent-based prohibitions on a narrow set of AI harms
- 60-day cure period (uniquely generous)
- NIST AI RMF / ISO 42001 substantial compliance safe harbor
- AG-only enforcement (no private right of action)
- Sectoral carveouts for insurance and federally insured financial
  institutions
- Healthcare provider disclosure duty (broad private-sector
  reach)
- Optional sandbox program

### What TRAIGA IS NOT (correcting common misconceptions)

- NOT a broad private-sector consumer disclosure law (only
  governmental and healthcare have disclosure duties)
- NOT a duty to test for AI bias (intent-based, not disparate
  impact)
- NOT subject to class actions (no private right of action)
- NOT applicable to employment-context interactions in the
  "consumer" definition (commercial and employment contexts
  excluded)
