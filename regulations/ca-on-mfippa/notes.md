- Statute base: live since 1990

  - Royal Assent: April 23, 2026
  - Procedural amendments: in force July 1, 2026
  - Privacy amendments (PIA, breach, IPC powers): in force January 1, 2027
  - Whistleblower protections: in force July 1, 2026

## Confidence calibration
- Average confidence: 0.90
- Highest: Whistleblower (0.92) — clearly drafted in Bill 97 text
- Lowest: IPC binding orders (0.87) — exact scope developing

## Why MFIPPA matters strategically for Sentainel
1. **Brand-new statutory regime** — Bill 97 passed three weeks ago
   (April 23, 2026). Almost no competitor has this in their product
   yet. First-mover window: 6-12 months.
2. **Forces every Ontario municipality to act** — 444 municipalities
   + 72 school boards + 245 library boards + 50 boards of health
   + ~150 other institutions = 961 potential customers
3. **AI is the worst-case PIA scenario** — Most municipalities have
   never done a PIA. Their first one will likely be for an AI system.
   Sentainel is positioned to be the default tool.
4. **Vendor flow-down doubles TAM** — Every Sentainel-using
   municipality requires its AI vendors to provide compliance
   evidence. Vendors become the second sale.

## Companion legislation tracking
- **Bill 194 / EDSTA** (Enhancing Digital Security and Trust Act) —
  AI accountability framework, regulations pending mid-to-late 2026
- **Bill 149** (Working for Workers Four Act) — applies to
  municipalities with 25+ employees using AI in hiring
- **PHIPA** — applies to municipal public health services
- **Ontario Human Rights Code** — uncapped discrimination teeth
- **AODA** — accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA) for all municipal digital
  services including AI
- **Federal PIPEDA / CPPA** — applies where commercial activity
  involved
