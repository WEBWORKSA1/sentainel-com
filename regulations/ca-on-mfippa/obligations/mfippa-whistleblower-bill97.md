required_action: |
  Municipal institutions shall not retaliate against any employee,
  agent, or contractor who, in good faith, reports a suspected
  contravention of MFIPPA to the Information and Privacy Commissioner
  of Ontario. Institutions shall establish internal mechanisms
  enabling staff to raise privacy concerns without fear of reprisal.
  The IPC will receive whistleblower reports confidentially.
plain_language_summary: |
  Effective July 1, 2026, municipal staff can confidentially report
  privacy or AI-related compliance failures directly to the IPC.
  Retaliation against whistleblowers is prohibited. Institutions
  must build internal channels for raising concerns.

  CRITICAL FOR AI: This is the operational lever that converts
  PIA and breach obligations from theoretical to actual. An IT
  staffer who knows their employer is using an unauthorized AI tool
  in violation of MFIPPA can now bypass internal politics and report
  directly to the IPC.
trigger_conditions:
  - condition: Subject institution
    formal: Any MFIPPA institution under s. 2(1).
  - condition: Internal reporting mechanism required
    formal: |
      Institutions must create accessible internal channels enabling
      staff to confidentially raise privacy and AI compliance
      concerns.
  - condition: Good-faith external report to IPC
    formal: |
      Reports to the IPC made in good faith (subjective belief that
      a contravention occurred, with reasonable basis) trigger
      whistleblower protections.
evidence_required:

  - Staff training on whistleblower rights
  - Records of internal reports received and dispositions
  - Anti-retaliation policy with clear consequences
  - Reporting accessible to contractors and vendors, not just

  - Reputational damage from public IPC reports
